# About Me

I am building toward AI Product Manager / Technical Product Manager roles from a background that combines early-stage AI experimentation, agent workflow design, technical product leadership, and customer-driven product development.

I have been working on AI agent concepts since before the current agent tooling wave became mainstream. In the early stages of this work, I consulted with startups on AI projects focused on practical early adoption of agentic systems and LLM-driven workflows. That work gave me an early view into a challenge that still defines the space today: the gap between exciting model capability and reliable product execution.

From there, I began working on workflow automation systems similar in spirit to the kinds of agent-building and orchestration platforms now gaining broad adoption. As part of that period, I worked on a platform called **agentic.build**, which was designed around AI-driven workflow creation and automation before being sunsetted. During that time, I also built AI agents for business plan generation and grant application creation, using tools and frameworks such as **LangChain** and **Hugging Face** while training and adapting LLM-based systems for these domain-specific tasks.

I later moved into **Quote n Go**, where I served as **CTO** and led the AI-focused product direction behind a pricing and estimation platform for home services. The product used satellite imagery and property-level analysis to estimate time-to-completion for maintenance and improvement work. It also incorporated material costs, contractor pricing, and industry-specific service logic to generate market-aligned price estimates for services such as driveway paving, lawn mowing, roofing, gutter cleaning, and other home maintenance categories. I led the work around the predictive models and technical direction that supported those estimates, and we successfully launched an MVP.

Today, at **STAN AI**, I work deeply in the product space at the intersection of customer needs, operational constraints, and technical execution. A major part of my value comes from bridging what customers actually need with what development teams can realistically build, sequence, and support. I enjoy operating in that middle layer where product quality is shaped not only by ideas, but by prioritization, implementation reality, workflow design, and cross-functional alignment.

Across these experiences, a clear theme has emerged in how I think about product:

- strong AI products are not just model demos
- value comes from turning technical capability into reliable user outcomes
- product quality depends on workflow design, observability, evaluation, fallback logic, and execution discipline
- the best product work sits at the intersection of user pain, system design, and business reality

That is the perspective I bring to product management, especially in AI-heavy environments where success depends on much more than the model itself.
