# Pricing / Operations / Adoption Case Study

## Overview
This case study outlines how I would evaluate a product opportunity where pricing, operational delivery, and user adoption are tightly connected.

## Problem
A product can deliver real value but still struggle with adoption if:
- pricing is not clearly tied to customer outcomes
- operational friction slows implementation
- users do not fully understand the value
- internal teams cannot consistently support rollout

## Users Affected
- customers evaluating whether the product is worth the price
- account teams trying to position value
- operations teams supporting adoption
- product leadership trying to scale efficiently

## Product Goals
- improve adoption
- improve customer understanding of value
- reduce implementation friction
- strengthen retention and expansion potential
- align pricing more clearly with realized outcomes

## Key Product Questions
1. what customer pain is expensive enough to justify spend?
2. what usage threshold creates clear ROI?
3. what implementation blockers reduce time to value?
4. what product changes would improve adoption?
5. how should value be measured and communicated?

## Proposed Approach

### 1. Map value to customer outcomes
Tie pricing to time saved, risk reduced, or throughput improved.

### 2. Identify implementation friction
List the onboarding, setup, and operational blockers that delay adoption.

### 3. Improve product communication
Clarify the product’s value in concrete operational terms.

### 4. Track adoption by stage
Measure:
- activated users
- engaged users
- repeat usage
- retained usage
- expansion opportunities

### 5. Build feedback loops into roadmap planning
Use adoption and churn signals to prioritize product improvements.

## Metrics
- activation rate
- time to first value
- weekly active usage
- retention rate
- expansion rate
- adoption by cohort
- implementation time
- support burden during rollout

## Risks / Tradeoffs
- lower pricing can increase adoption but weaken margin
- higher pricing can reduce adoption unless value is explicit
- operational complexity can undermine otherwise strong product value
- sales promises can create product expectation gaps

## PM Skills Demonstrated
- commercial reasoning
- customer-value mapping
- operational product thinking
- adoption strategy
- metrics-based decision making
